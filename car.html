VD-01 Car

The Veloce Dynamics race car for the 2025/2026 season: designed, modelled and refined through simulations and feedback.

Overview

Design Philosophy

The VD-01 is designed around aerodynamic efficiency, structural integrity and rule compliance. The modelling team balances minimal drag with practical considerations such as manufacturability and robustness under race conditions.

CAD and simulations are iterated in cycles, using feedback from test data and physics analysis to refine shapes such as wings, nose, underbody and wheels.

Aerodynamics
Streamlined profile, optimised cross-section and controlled airflow around the wheels to reduce drag and turbulence.
CFD & Testing
Multiple CFD runs to compare configurations, evaluate pressure distribution and track improvements in drag reduction.
Manufacturing
Designed for accurate machining / 3D printing, with attention to tolerances and surface finish where it matters.
Physics Input
Use of physics-based reasoning for forces, acceleration and race strategy, aligning design with measurable performance.

Development

From first sketches to the final car used at the competition, every step is documented in the Engineering and Project Management documents.

Concept & Sketches

Initial Ideas

Early car concepts explore different body shapes, wing placements and side pod configurations while respecting rules and focussing on low drag and stability.

Detailed visuals and justification for the final concept are available in the Engineering document.

Simulation & Iteration

Testing & Refinement

CFD simulations inform decisions on where to remove material, add features or adjust angles. Results are interpreted alongside physics calculations to ensure changes are meaningful.
